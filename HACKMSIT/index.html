DERMATOLOGIST

SKIN DOCTOR

A dermatologist is a doctor that specializes in treating skin, hair, nail, and mucous membrane disorders. A dermatologist can also provide support for cosmetic issues, helping patients to revitalize the appearance of their skin, hair, and nails. Dermatology is an area of medicine concerned with the health of the skin and diseases of the hair, nails, and mucous membranes.

ORTHOPEDIC

BONE DOCTOR

For diseases and conditions of the bones, muscles, ligaments, tendons, and joints. You may have symptoms of:
• arthritis
• sports injuries
• bone tumors
• back pain
• broken bones
• osteoporosis
• repetitive strain injuries, such as carpal tunnel syndrome or tennis elbow

ONCOLOGIST

CANCER DOCTOR

For treatment of cancer and its symptoms. During treatment for cancer, you may have several types of healthcare professional in their care team. Some common symptoms are:
• Breast Cancer Symptoms of breast cancer include a lump in the breast, bloody discharge from the nipple and changes in the shape or texture of the nipple or breast.
• Prostate Cancer Symptoms include difficulty with urination, but sometimes there are no symptoms at all.
• Skin Cancer Symptoms might include a new, unusual growth or a change in an existing mole. Melanomas can occur anywhere on the body.

PSYCHIATRISTS

MENTAL DOCTOR

A psychiatrist is a doctor who treats mental health conditions. They may use counseling, medication, or hospitalization as part of their treatment.

NEUROLOGIST

NERVES DOCTOR

A neurologist treats conditions of the nerves, spine, and brain. You may consult a neurologist for:
• epilepsy
• Alzheimer’s disease
• Parkinson’s disease
• multiple sclerosis
• neuropathy
• stroke
• migraine

OTOLARYNGOLOGISTS

ENT DOCTOR

Otolaryngologists are sometimes known as “ear, nose, and throat” (ENT) for treatments of sinuses, throat, tonsils, ears, mouth, head, and neck.

CARDIOLOGISTS

HEART DOCTOR

Cardiologists focus on the cardiovascular system, which includes the heart and blood vessels. They treat many cardiovascular conditions, such as:
• high blood pressure
• high cholesterol
• heart attack and stroke
• heart rhythm problems
• congestive heart failure

PEDIATRICIAN

CHILD DOCTOR

For treating children from birth to young adulthood, rathe taking overall care and development of the child looking for:
• well-baby and well-child visits
• school or sports physicals
• childhood vaccines
• disease prevention for children and babies
• treating illnesses and injuries in children and babies

OBSTETRICIAN

FEMALE DOCTOR

Obstetrician/gynecologists (OB/GYNs) provide preventive care and disease management for female health conditions. These include:
• female reproductive health
• cancer prevention and diagnosis in the female reproductive organs
• breast care
• pregnancy
• labor and delivery
Many women will see an OB/GYN regularly throughout pregnancy and for labor and delivery. They may also visit them for yearly checkups and tests, such as breast exams and Pap tests.

NEPHROLOGISTS

KIDNEY DOCTOR

For kidney care and conditions that affect the kidneys such as:
• kidney disease
• renal (kidney) failure
• diabetes
• kidney stones
• lupus
• hypertension
• Nephrologists also have education in kidney transplants and dialysis.
