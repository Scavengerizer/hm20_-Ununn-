Q: What should i do about extremely watery eyes?


Watery eyes’ suggests two possible problems:excess tear production and/or poor drainage. Excess production is usually in response to irritation. Irritation is aa total of all the irritants getting into the eyes; a problem rather like dealing with debt! Some irritants will be obvious, especially if they are recent, but many will be due to long term habits, and difficult to identify, because they are due to ‘normal behaviour’. The topic is potentially vast: it includes smoke (from cooking, smoking, fire places, and air pollution), aerosols (from ‘air fresheners’, cleaners, paints), wind, especially cycling without eye protection, dust (especially from hobbies like carpentry), solvents, make-up and make-up removers, and shampoo or just water from the shower. Poor drainage is due to blockage of the tear ducts, which drain the tears from the inner corner of each eye lid. These can be unblocked by syringing, performed by an ophthalmologist.
